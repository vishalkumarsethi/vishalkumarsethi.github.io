I must say that this new "foodplan" I am trying it is pretty interesting. I was advised that high protein is ok so long as you consume large amounts of water (and it doesn't hurt to exercise as well). Noted.

Essentially, we have eggs/oatmeal for breakfast, beef or chicken for lunch and then dinner is the same as lunch. Along with baby carrots, salad, fruits (citrus, bananas, blueberries), and some seasonings/sauces, we have a very simple but sufficient food plan. Definitely not as "satiating" as a high carb or "outside-food" diet, but most people agree that carbs can be heavy as well as no outside food brings total food costs down considerably. $10 for a meal is CRAZY.

Thoughts: 

I was thinking about food/eating/diet/cost/efficiency/health a bit earlier. Most people would agree that they WANT to be healthy, so they are receptive to changes/ideas about how to improve health. Food seems to play an important part since we seem to need it to live, so we'll start here. We'll assume that drinkable water is readily available, along with other basic resources.

Side note: the water/food problem is still on the way to being solved; in an alternate universe I would help with that but not for this life unfortunately.  Maybe in my lifetime? Hopefully. I'm not a food scientist but I take it GMOs and new tech will help with that. 

Water should be consumed in decently high volumes on the basis that our bodies contain/run on water, there is a lot of water on the earth, and that if humans don't drink it in low amounts we die. Sounds reasonable. 

I would imagine "recommended" amounts/values for MOST THINGS by the USDA/FDA/other health organizations are reputable and good, but they are just that. Good. It is hard for a large majority of the population to take a great recommendation if it is much harder e.g. If we ran a trial for people to drink 40oz, 60oz, 80oz, 100oz of water...compliance would decrease as you increase volume? I still think this makes sense. 

Enough about water. 

A stove, pan, microwave, and some utensils can be used with a diet consisting of chicken, rice, veggies (frozen or salad), and supplementary items. Get some meat, carbs, veggies, fruits (citrus, blueberries). Sounds "balanced" and rather easy. 

Eating the same thing every day, given that this food plan (diet isn't the right word IMO) has necessary macro/micronutrients is probably pretty good. Predictable, efficient, rather easy. 

Of course, it gets "boring" since humans thrive on novelty in EVERY aspect of life...so we decorate with seasoning/salt and sauces. Calories, sodium intake is whatever. Generally humans seem to have problems with TOO MUCH OR TOO LITTLE of things...so I guess "moderation is key" is very valuable. 

There is always more to say, but for another time. 

Also, if you make chicken on a pan, be careful. Tenderloins will sear quicker then they will cook on the inside if on high heat. Breasts and other chicken/meats vary but should be better...usually.
